Can marijuana affect teen brain development?

WHAT WE KNOW: the prefrontal cortex does not reach full maturity until the early twenties. leaving may complex processes of the adolescent brain underdeveloped. Neuroscientific research indicates that during this time, teens are especially vulnerable to the effects of cannabis because of the unique nature of their young brains. Delaying or preventing the onset of regular marijuana use from early teen years to adulthood can prevent long-term neuronal damage and ensure optimal brain health and functioning.
